In the Footsteps of Great Photographers

A tribute to the defining creators of photography — through a special year-end exhibition.

We have all dreamed of being someone's muse — a model, a character, the protagonist of a beautifully composed image. Imagine seeing something in a photograph of yourself that you never thought possible.

During the project, we reveal different sides of each participant through visual references to influential photographers. Two selected portraits are combined into one 50 × 70 cm lenticular artwork. The images are divided into very fine strips and precisely aligned beneath a micro-lens surface. As a result, a different portrait becomes visible from each viewing angle: when you walk past the artwork, one image changes into the other. It is a physical optical print — not a screen or digital animation. Read the international overview of lenticular printing on Wikipedia.

You can be understated or extravagant, mysterious or expressive — what matters is that you bring yourself.

Frequently asked questions.

The essential points, without ambiguous fine print.

When is the contract formed?

Not when the application form is submitted. The contract is formed only through the separate confirmation sent after selection; that message contains the payment link and final participation terms.

What does the EUR 1,200 total value mean?

It is the combined estimated professional and production value of the shoot, retouching, custom lenticular production, exhibition participation and opening event. Through the VIPACH community project, the participant pays a EUR 299 material and production cost contribution.

What happens to the exhibited artwork?

The personalised 50 × 70 cm lenticular artwork becomes the participant’s property after the exhibition.

Must my images be used for marketing?

No. The use required for the exhibition is part of the project and is documented in a separate model/exhibition agreement. Advertising or media use beyond that requires separate, voluntary permission.

Do I have a right of withdrawal?

Consumers generally have a 14-day withdrawal right for distance contracts. A statutory exception may apply to the individually manufactured lenticular artwork made to personal specifications; see the withdrawal information for details.
